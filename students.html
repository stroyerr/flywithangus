First solo

Foundations

Build consistent aircraft control, traffic-pattern judgement, radio discipline, go-around decisions, and safe independence before flying alone.

Training focus
  • Preflight preparation and aircraft documents
  • Normal, crosswind, and go-around procedures
  • Emergency procedures and sound judgement

  • Cross-country planning and diversions

Instrument

Rating

Instrument training

IFR systems

Develop disciplined instrument flying, procedural awareness, weather judgement, clearance management, and workload control in the IFR system.

Training focus
  • Attitude-instrument flying and automation management
  • Clearances, departures, arrivals, holds, and approaches
  • IFR weather, alternates, fuel, and missed-approach decisions
Official references
§ 61.65 Instrument rating ↗ Instrument Rating ACS ↗ Instrument Procedures Handbook ↗

Commercial

Commercial pilot training

Professionalism

Refine precision, energy management, aircraft mastery, aeronautical judgement, and professional consistency beyond private-pilot standards.

Training focus
  • Commercial maneuvers and energy management
  • Advanced systems, performance, and operational decisions
  • Professional briefings, standards, and self-evaluation

Flight reviews & proficiency

Continuous learning

Keep legal currency separate from genuine proficiency and use recurrent training to address the risks, missions, and aircraft that matter to your flying.

Training focus
  • Scenario-based decision-making and risk management
  • Emergency, abnormal, and proficiency work
  • Instrument currency, flight reviews, and WINGS activities
